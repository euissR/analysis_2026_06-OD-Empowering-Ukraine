Empowering Ukraine, Strengthening Europe

edited by Ondrej Ditrych, Senior Analyst, EUISS

More than three years into Russia's full-scale invasion, Ukraine's survival is no longer the only question on the table — its long-term empowerment is. That shift demands more than emergency support: it calls for a coherent European strategy that spans sanctions enforcement, defence-industrial cooperation, enlargement, and reconstruction alike.

This collection brings together eleven chapters from EU policy analysts, each examining a distinct front in that effort — from closing sanctions loopholes on Russian energy and countering Kremlin recruitment abroad, to deepening joint arms production, financing reconstruction, and getting enlargement right.

Together, they set out a practical agenda for turning wartime solidarity into structural support — one that outlasts the current phase of the war and gives Ukraine the tools to shape its own recovery.
