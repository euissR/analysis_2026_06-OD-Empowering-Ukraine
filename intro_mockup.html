Deep strikes

 

Since the beginning of the war, 96% of Ukraine's strikes (air and drone strikes as well as shellings, artillery and missile attacks) took place within 100km of its borders.

 

However, Ukraine's long-range campaign has ramped up significantly over the course of the war. While in 2022, it contudcted a modest 5 strikes 100km or more beyond it's borders...

 

...that figure increased 14-fold in 2023...

 

...and a further four-fold to 299 in 2024.

 

The current annual peak of 661 in 2025...

 

...is set to be eclipsed in 2026, with nearly 400 events reported by mid-June.

 
 
 

Given the steady increase in Ukrainian strikes on Russian territory...

 

...the number of long-range strikes seems almost insignificant.

 

However, upon closer inspection, year-on-year growth proves to be marked - and generally above the growth rates of strikes closer to the border.

 
 

Destruction and recovery

 

Russian attacks on civilian infrastructure have increased over time.

 

Since the beginning of the war, an average 70% of Russian attacks hit residential infrastructure. Meanwhile, the targeting of energy infrastructure, after a relatively lean 2023, has become a mainstay of Russian attacks.

 

Frontline areas - and Melitopol and the Donbass in particular - bear the brunt of attacks on civilian infrastructure.

 

This holds true particularly for attacks on energy infrastructure.

 

Beyond the frontline, Kharkiv, Sumy and Kyiv figure prominently.

 

Frontline settlements are hit particularly hard where entire villages and towns have been obliterated by relentless attacks.

 
 

This translates into significant destruction.
