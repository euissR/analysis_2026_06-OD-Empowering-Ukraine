Deep strikes

 

Since the beginning of the war, 96% of Ukraine's strikes (air and drone strikes as well as shellings, artillery and missile attacks) took place within 100km of its borders.

 

However, Ukraine's long-range campaign has ramped up significantly over the course of the war. While in 2022, it contudcted a modest 5 strikes 100km or more beyond it's borders, that figure increased 14-fold in 2023 and a further four-fold to about 300 in 2024 - nearly one for every day.

 

The current annual peak of 661 in 2025 is set to be eclipsed in 2026, with nearly 400 events reported by mid-June.

Destruction and recovery

 

Attacks on infrastructure have hit new highs in 2025, roughly tripling the monthly average to 170 attacks. Yet, 2026 is set to surpass that figure with 250 monthly attacks thus far.

 

Since the beginning of the war, an average 70% of Russian attacks hit residential infrastructure. Meanwhile, the targeting of energy infrastructure, after a relatively lean 2023, has become a mainstay of Russian attacks.

 
 

This translates into significant destruction.

 

By the end of 2025, the total damage (to what exactly???) was estimated at $195 billion. Donetska oblast accounts for about one quarter of that, and Kharkivska and Zaporizka oblasts together for another quarter.

 

Needs are multiples of that: nearly $600 billion for the whole of Ukraine, with Donetska, Kharkivska, Zaporizka and Kyivska oblasts almost accounting for half.
